R3D: Revisiting 3D Policy Learning

Anonymous Authors
Anonymous Institution
Paper Video Code arXiv

Abstract

3D policy learning promises superior generalization and cross-embodiment transfer, but progress has been hindered by training instabilities and severe overfitting, precluding the adoption of powerful 3D perception models. In this work, we systematically diagnose these failures, identifying the omission of 3D data augmentation and the adverse effects of Batch Normalization as primary causes. We propose a new architecture coupling a scalable transformer-based 3D encoder with a diffusion decoder, engineered specifically for stability at scale and designed to leverage large-scale pre-training. Our approach significantly outperforms state-of-the-art 3D baselines on challenging manipulation benchmarks, establishing a new and robust foundation for scalable 3D imitation learning.

01

Overview

While 2D-centric policies like Diffusion Policy and ACT have achieved impressive results, their reliance on 2D projections of an inherently 3D world introduces fundamental limitations in viewpoint generalization and cross-embodiment skill transfer. 3D policy learning addresses this by reasoning over canonical 3D scene representations, enabling natural multi-view fusion and inherent viewpoint invariance.

However, prior 3D policy methods face a "scaling paradox"—more powerful 3D backbones paradoxically lead to worse performance. We identify two critical overlooked causes: Batch Normalization degrading powerful 3D encoders, and the omission of 3D data augmentation causing severe overfitting. By systematically addressing these issues, R3D unlocks the true potential of scaled 3D policy learning.

02

Method

BN → LN

Replacing Batch Normalization with Layer Normalization resolves the scaling paradox, enabling powerful 3D encoders like Uni3D to be effectively trained for policy learning.

3D Data Augmentation

FPS randomization, point cloud color jitter, and additive noise with dropout stabilize training and significantly mitigate overfitting in 3D policy learning.

Spatially-Aware Decoding

Cross-attention between action queries and dense geometric tokens preserves spatial resolution, enabling precise action predictions for fine-grained manipulation.

3D Pretraining

Leveraging the encoder structure from 3D segmentation enables pretraining on large-scale datasets (ScanNet, ARKitScenes, PartNeXt), injecting rich geometric priors.

Multi-Objective Decoding

Jointly decoding end-effector poses and joint angles with causal attention masking provides proprioceptive grounding without interfering with primary control.

Dense Feature Conditioning

Unlike prior methods that collapse 3D features into a global vector, R3D preserves the full spatial resolution of patch tokens for fine-grained geometric reasoning.

Decoder Comparison. (a) DP3 uses a global feature and FiLM conditioning. (b) Our approach preserves dense spatial features and uses cross-attention for spatially-aware action denoising, enabling the model to attend to specific task-relevant regions during diffusion iterations.

Real-World Demos

We evaluate R3D on three real-world tasks using an xArm6 robot with two Intel RealSense D435 cameras. Each task tests different aspects of 3D spatial understanding: pose-constrained pick-and-place, articulated object manipulation, and soft-body manipulation.

Task 1: Place Kettle on Stove

A pose-constrained pick-and-place task requiring grasping at the thin handle and placing with strictly defined spatial poses. Tests fine-grained positional control.

76% Success Rate

Task 2: Open Drawer

An articulated-object manipulation task that challenges the policy's understanding of rigid body dynamics and spatial reasoning for handle grasping.

64% Success Rate

Task 3: Fold Towel

A soft-body manipulation task testing the policy's ability to handle deformable objects, requiring robust spatial understanding and adaptive motion planning.

66% Success Rate
