Abstract

3D policy learning promises superior generalization and cross-embodiment transfer, but progress has been hindered by training instabilities and severe overfitting, precluding the adoption of powerful 3D perception models. In this work, we systematically diagnose these failures, identifying the omission of 3D data augmentation and the adverse effects of Batch Normalization as primary causes. We propose a new architecture coupling a scalable transformer-based 3D encoder with a diffusion decoder, engineered specifically for stability at scale and designed to leverage large-scale pre-training. Our approach significantly outperforms state-of-the-art 3D baselines on challenging manipulation benchmarks, establishing a new and robust foundation for scalable 3D imitation learning.

Key Insights

Prior 3D policy methods face a "scaling paradox"—more powerful 3D backbones paradoxically lead to worse performance. We identify two critical overlooked causes and propose actionable training principles:

1

BN → Layer Norm

Batch Normalization degrades powerful 3D encoders under the small batch sizes typical of imitation learning. Replacing BN with Layer Normalization resolves the scaling paradox, enabling encoders like Uni3D to be effectively trained.

2

3D Data Augmentation

The common implementation of DP3 omits data augmentation, causing severe overfitting. Our pipeline of FPS randomization, point cloud color jitter, and additive noise with dropout stabilizes training and significantly improves success rates.

Architecture Design

Building on these insights, R3D integrates a high-capacity transformer-based 3D encoder with a Diffusion Transformer decoder, featuring several key design choices:

🔍

Spatially-Aware Decoding

Unlike DP3's global feature + FiLM conditioning, our decoder performs cross-attention directly between action queries and dense geometric tokens, preserving spatial resolution for precise manipulation.

🧠

3D Encoder Pretraining

Leveraging the encoder structure from PointSAM, we pretrain on large-scale 3D segmentation datasets (ScanNet, ARKitScenes, PartNeXt), injecting rich geometric priors that accelerate convergence.

🤖

Multi-Objective Decoding

We jointly decode end-effector poses and joint angles with causal attention masking, providing proprioceptive grounding without interfering with primary joint control.

Figure 2. Decoder Comparison. (a) DP3 uses a global feature and FiLM conditioning for the denoising process. (b) Our approach preserves dense spatial features and uses cross-attention for spatially-aware action denoising, enabling the model to attend to specific task-relevant regions during diffusion iterations.
